Salient Features

The Department of Information Technology was started in the year 1999 with an intake of 30 students and currently has a sanctioned intake of 120 students.

The B.Tech Information Technology program is accredited by NBA for the period 2022–2025. The department focuses on domain-specific training, research orientation, and holistic student development.

State-of-the-art laboratories support projects, consultancy, and research activities. The department received the AICTE–CII Award 2018 for Best Industry-Linked Institute.

Faculty Strength

The department has 24 faculty members comprising Professors, Associate Professors, and Assistant Professors. 50% of the faculty members are Ph.D. holders.

Faculty members actively engage in consultancy, research, and industry-driven training in areas such as AI, Data Science, Full Stack Engineering, Cloud Computing, and Cyber Security.

Curriculum Enrichment

Virtusa contributes to 40.38% of the curriculum design. The curriculum emphasizes practicum-integrated courses, expert-led instruction, and AI-driven learning through RMK NextGen.

Research & Development

Faculty and students publish extensively in reputed journals and conferences. The department collaborates with international universities and industries for funded research and advanced AI-based diagnostic systems.

Professional Societies & Student Activities

Students actively participate in professional bodies such as IEI, ISTE, IETE, IEEE, and the IT Students Association. The annual national-level symposium CYBERNAUTIX provides a platform for technical excellence.

Centres of Excellence

The department houses four Centres of Excellence including Virtusa Full Stack, Front End, Cloud Computing, and Data Science CoEs, promoting industry-led innovation.
